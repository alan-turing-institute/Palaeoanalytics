========================================================================================

1. METHODOLOGICAL ADVANTAGES (with quantification)
   - Speed: "PyLithics processes X lithics in Y minutes vs Z hours manually"
   - Consistency: "Inter-observer variability reduced by X%"
   - Scale: "Enables analysis of thousands of lithics vs dozens manually"
   - Novel metrics: "Scar complexity and symmetry not feasible to measure manually"

2. ARCHAEOLOGICAL IMPLICATIONS
   - Research questions now addressable at scale:
     * Technological change over time
     * Geographic variation in techniques
     * Skill and learning curves
     * Raw material use strategies
   - Meta-analysis potential: reanalyze published assemblages
   - Integration with other data (paleoenvironmental, genetic, etc.)

3. LIMITATIONS AND APPROPRIATE USE
   - When to use: flakes, good quality illustrations, large samples
   - When NOT to use: cores, thick tools, heavily retouched pieces, poor images
   - Trade-offs: time for illustration vs automation gains
   - Validation requirements for new assemblages/regions

4. COMPARISON TO ALTERNATIVES

   vs Manual Measurement:
   + Speed, consistency, novel metrics, scalability
   - Requires illustration time, limited to 2D

   vs 3D Scanning:
   + Cost, accessibility, historical data, speed
   - Less accurate for thick pieces, limited depth info

   vs Other Computer Vision:
   + Open source, works with published data, comprehensive metrics
   - Requires line drawings (others work with photos)

5. UNIQUE CONTRIBUTION
   PyLithics enables:
   - Scale: thousands of lithics from published sources
   - Standardization: consistent, reproducible measurements
   - Novel metrics: not feasible manually (complexity, symmetry, Voronoi)
   - Accessibility: free, open-source, works with existing publications
   - Integration: combines typological and technological data
